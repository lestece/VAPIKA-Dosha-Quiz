Ayurveda Fundamentals

Ayurveda is an ancient holistic healing system developed in India more than 3,000 years ago.

It's based on the principle that the 5 elements - Earth, Fire, Water, Air and Space - found in the Universe also exist within our bodies.

These elements combine in varying degrees to form the so-called "doshas" (also known as energetic forces of nature or functional principles): they are Vata (Air and Space), Pitta (Fire and Water) and Kapha (Water and Earth).

Each person has a varying amount of each element and a unique combination of the doshas, that describe one's unique constitution (Prakruti in Ayurveda).

Ayurveda's main purpose is to promote good health and prevent disease, revolving around the idea that health and wellness depend on a delicate balance between the body, mind and spirit.

The elements govern the way our body responds to the outside influences: food, habits, weather, environment.
So the more you learn about the doshas and your Prakruti, the more you are able to make the right choices to bring the body back to a state of health and balance!

How to take the test & what to do with the results

  1. Read each question carefully and choose the answer that best describes you.
  2. Answer each question as objectively and honestly as possible, focusing on what has been true for you throughout your life. If you are struggling with a choice, choose according to what best reflects your healthiest, most vibrant period of life.
  3. The quiz result will probably show that you have a dominant dosha, and a second one that is less obvious. So for example, you would be Pitta-Vata, Kapha-Pitta etc. You should generally focus on balancing your dominant dosha and following its guidelines, but also always being aware of your second dosha's tendencies and signs of imbalance.
  4. If you end up with a Tridoshic result (when you have an even amount of all of the three doshas), it means you have a fairly balanced constitution. If this is the case, Ayurveda suggests that you follow a Vata diet and its guidelines because it's the first dosha that goes out of balance.
